# Finance podcast transcript specification

Use this reference when the user asks for a podcast script based on the latest Stock Review Hub daily review.

## Source discipline

- Use the latest completed and verified daily review as the factual source of truth.
- Preserve the review date and distinguish market facts from host interpretation.
- Do not add prices, macro figures, earnings data, rumors, or social sentiment that the review does not support.
- If a necessary fact is missing, omit it or say that the data is unavailable. Never substitute `0` or a template value.
- Keep tracking ideas separate from personalized investment advice.

## Audience and voice

- Write for listeners aged roughly 20–48 with broad interest in finance, markets, technology, and current affairs.
- Sound like a top financial-current-affairs podcast host: informed, conversational, skeptical, and precise.
- Explain why a number matters instead of reading tables aloud.
- Use vivid but restrained language. Avoid hype, guaranteed outcomes, slogans, and empty motivational phrasing.
- Prefer natural spoken Mandarin unless the user requests another language.

## Required structure

Write the transcript in this order:

1. **Show greeting** — welcome listeners and identify the review date.
2. **Opening market question** — frame the session with one sharp question or contradiction.
3. **Core investor problem awareness** — explain the decision problem facing ordinary investors, such as whether a rally reflects improving fundamentals, easier liquidity, or crowded positioning.
4. **Key review information** — narrate the most important index, macro, industry-chain, stock, sentiment, risk, and quant findings from the review.
5. **Deep comment on the controversy** — examine the strongest bull and bear interpretations, state what evidence would falsify each side, and give the host’s evidence-based view.
6. **Next-session watch** — identify the specific data releases, price levels, sectors, or companies that could confirm or reject the thesis.
7. **Not-investment-advice close** — close the show and clearly state that the discussion is for information and research, not personalized investment advice.

## Format and pacing

- Default to an 8–12 minute episode, roughly 2,200–3,200 Chinese characters, unless the user specifies a duration.
- Produce a true word-for-word script, not an outline.
- Use short paragraphs, spoken transitions, and optional cues such as `[停顿]` sparingly.
- Give the episode a concise title and include the source review date.
- Do not expose internal Stage labels, chain-of-thought, or production notes.

## Final quality check

- Confirm that every market figure appears in the source review.
- Confirm that the opening question is answered by the end.
- Include both the bull case and the countercase.
- Include concrete next-session watch items.
- End with an explicit non-investment-advice disclaimer.
